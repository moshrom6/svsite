Professions

Professions are selected between 2 choices at Skill Lvl. (level) 5, & 2 more at Skill Lvl. 10 (for context, Skills are player attributes which are leveled up through the use of specific tools or actions which provide xp, a.k.a. experience points, or by reading certain Books). The Professions given to choose from at Skill Lvl. 10 are based on what Profession was previously chosen at Lvl. 5, resulting in 4 potential Professions at Lvl. 10 per Skill (something worth considering before deciding). Some Professions that seem helpful to me are shown below:

Farming Profession(s)
Lvl. 5 - Tiller (Crops worth 10% more gold)
Lvl. 10 - Artisan (Artisan goods worth 40% more gold)
or Lvl. 10 - Agriculturist (All crops grow 10% faster)
Foraging Profession(s)
Lvl. 5 - Gatherer (Chance for double harvest of foraged goods)
Lvl. 10 - Botanist (Foraged goods are always Iridium quality)
Fishing Profession(s)
Lvl. 5 - Trapper (Resources required to craft crab pots reduced)
Lvl. 10 - Mariner (Crab pots no longer produce junk)
Mining Profession(s)
Lvl. 5 - Geologist (Chance for gems to appear in pairs)
Lvl. 10 - Excavator (Chance to find geodes doubled)
Combat Profession(s)
Lvl. 5 - Scout (Critical Strike chance increased by 50%)
Lvl. 10 - Acrobat (Cooldown for Special Moves cut in half)

Skills & XP

Each Skill has 10 Lvls. Skills must be leveled up with xp, requiring more xp per Lvl., additively. For example, the Book Of Stars provides +250xp for all 5 Skills when read. Below are a few ways to earn xp for each Skill:

Farming Skill
Skill xp for Farming is gained by harvesting crops, pricier crops giving more xp, by petting, milking, or shearing farm animals (+5xp each), or by picking up an animal product inside a barn or coop (+5xp).
Foraging Skill
The Foraging Skill may be trained by gathering foraged items found on the ground throughout Stardew Valley, by chopping down trees with an Axe (+14xp), as well as a few other ways. The most xp-rich actions, in regard to Foraging, are reading a copy of the Woodcutter's Weekly (+250xp), removing large stumps & large logs (+25xp), destroying a large Green Rain weed (+15xp), and digging up a Seed Spot or Artifact Spot (each +15xp).
Fishing Skill
Any trash, green algae, white algae, seaweed, river jelly, sea jelly, or cave jelly caught with the fishing pole provide +3xp each, and crab pots provide +5xp each time they're collected from no matter what. Fishing xp gained from Fish Ponds works a bit differently (idk how yet). Otherwise, xp gained from fish is calculated from a formula based on the fish's quality & difficulty to catch. Xp is multiplied x2.2 if the player also catches a treasure chest, x2.4 if the catch was "perfect", and by 5 if the catch was a Legendary fish. These multiplications stack (which is a big incentive for "perfect" catches, especially when combined with the other 2 multipliers)! Reading Bait And Bobber also happens to increase Fishing Skill by +250xp.
Mining Skill
Mining Skill xp may be gained from mining rocks, boulders, & nodes, varying in xp gain. Regular looking rocks inside The Mines provide no xp on their own, but if coal is dropped, +5xp is awarded. Each piece of ore gained from panning with a pan provides one xp.
Combat Skill
Combat xp is increased by fighting various monsters (with varying xp gain). Look out for any Putrid Ghosts (+25xp when killed) or Iridium Bats (+22xp when killed), as they provide the most Combat xp when slain. Carbon Ghosts, Iridium Crabs, Mummies, Serpent/Royal Serpents, Tiger Slimes & Shadow Snipers each provide +20xp each when slain, making them worth murdering as well :D. Reading a copy of Combat Quarterly provides +250xp.

Mastery

Once max Lvl. (Lvl. 10) in all Skills have been reached, a door in Cindersap Forest may be used to enter the Mastery Cave. Here, any extra xp may be used to increase Mastery Points. Keep in mind, Farming xp only contributes to Mastery Points at a 50% rate, while xp for the rest of the Skills contribute to the Mastery Points at a normal 100% rate. Good luck, Master :P

Winter

Come Winter time, powdermelon are the only crop (not planted in the greenhouse) that will survive. They may grow giant :P

Giant Crops

At the start of each day, every possible 3x3 grid of the same type of crop has a 1% chance to fuse into a giant crop, as long as the top left crop is fully-grown and watered. Giant crops won't necessarily appear on the day the crops finish growing; it can happen any day the criteria is met. Giant crops drop 15–21 normal-quality items when harvested with any axe, taking three hits. It's good to know that giant crops cannot grow in the greenhouse, by using garden pots, or on Ginger Island. The following crops may grow giant:

  • Cauliflowers
  • Melons
  • Pumpkins
  • Powdermelons
  • & Qi fruits

Fruit Trees

Given a 3x3 untilled space to grow for 28 days (when mature), fruit trees produce one fruit per day (when in season), which can accumulate for up to three days before harvesting. A tree's fruit increases in quality by one star per year of tree age (after matured). Fruit trees grow in any season, do not need to be watered, and will not die in the Winter. Tree fertilizer cannot be used on fruit trees. Fruit trees will not be affected by weather, i.e., fruit will keep producing once per day, whether it is raining, windy, or sunny. However, during storms, fruit trees can be struck by lightning, causing them to become 'burnt' for 4 days (causing them to produce coal). Fruit bore from fruit trees are used in the Artisan, Fodder, & Enchanter's Bundles. Below is a rundown on the 8 different fruit trees:

Animals

There are various animals in Stardew Valley, including unobtainable wildlife, hostile entities, a pet chosen while creating a character, a rideable horse, and farm animals that produce resources. A Pet License may be obtained from Marnie's Ranch to adopt more pets after reaching max frienship with your first pet.

Farm Animals

Farm Animals may be raised in a coop, a barn, or a slime hutch (to raise slimes)

Coop Animals

These animals require a coop to live in. They'll leave their products inside the coop each morning, leaving better produce based on how happy they are. There are 4 unique coop animals (excluding the variants of chickens):

Blue / Brown / White Chickens
These regular chickens may be purchased from Marnie's Ranch for 800g (except void & Golden chickens, they cannot be bought), or hatched by placing an egg into an incubator (takes roughly 6d 16h in-game to incubate). After seeing Shane's 8-heart event, each Chicken purchaced from Marnie's Ranch has a 25% chance of being Blue. Produces a white/brown egg/large egg every day, depending on its color (Blue Chickens produce White eggs/large eggs).
Void Chickens
Void chickens live in a Big Coop. The only way to obtain a void chicken is through incubating a void egg. There are 3 ways to obtain a void egg: 1. Through a random event involving a Witch (she's a nice Witch), 2. Purchacing one from Krobus for 5,000g, or 3. Shane gifting a void egg after marrying him. Produces 1 void egg each day.
Golden Chickens
Not only do they require a Big Coop, but Perfection must be attained to acquire a golden chicken. After reaching Perfection, A golden egg may be purchased from Marnie's Ranch for 100,000g, or from Qi's Walnut Room for 100something (idk what currency yet). Also (after Perfection), A Witch may leave a golden egg in a coop overnight, and fishing treasure chests may contain them at this point in the game. Produces 1 golden egg every day.
Ducks
Ducks live in a Big Coop, and may be purchaced from Marnie's Ranch for 1,200g or be hatched by placing a duck egg into an incubator. An adult Duck produces a duck egg or a duck feather every other day, with a higher chance of duck feathers based on how happy they are.
Rabbits
Rabbits live in a Deluxe Coop, and may be purchased at Marnie's Ranch for 8,000g. They cannot reproduce. An adult Rabbit produces wool every 4 days, with a chance of producing a rabbit's foot if sufficient Friendship & Happiness is reached.
Dinosaurs
Dinosaurs require a Big Coop. To obtain a Dino, a dinosaur egg must be found, and then placed in an incubator. In approximately 12.5 in-game days, a Dino will hatch. Dinos like any other coop animal, but they are silent, and may lay a dinosaur egg worth 350g every 7 days.
A dinosaur egg may be found in various ways: By digging up an Artifact Spot in The Mountains (including the Quarry), in fishing treasure chests after reaching Fishing Lvl. 2 (and having previously found at least one artifact), dropped from a Pepper Rex, foraged on Prehistoric Floors in the Skull Cavern, or won in the Crane Game in the Movie Theater. A dino egg can also be gifted by a pet dog you have max friendship with :D

Barn Animals

These animals require a Barn to live in. Barn animals (except Ostrichs) may become pregnant at random if the pregnancy option is enabled on their status menu. For pregnancy to happen the player needs to have upgraded to at least a Big Barn and have enough room for another animal.

Cows
May live in any sized Barn. Cows can be purchased at Marnie's Ranch for 1,500g. They come in two varieties at random: White and Brown. Adult Cows will produce milk daily. If they reach higher friendship, they will produce large milk. Milk & large milk may be collected with a milk pail.
Goats
Lives in a Big Barn. Goats can be purchased at Marnie's Ranch for 4,000g. Goats typically produce goat milk every other day, and once sufficient friendship and happiness are reached, they will produce large goat milk. Goat milk may be harvested with a milk pail, similar to Cows.
Sheep
Lives in a Deluxe Barn. Sheep can be purchased at Marnie's Ranch for 8,000g. A mature sheep grows in its coat every 3 days, if it is fed and has at least 70 happiness (Note: The Shepherd Profession combined with at least 4.5 Hearts of Friendship with a Sheep will make it capable of producing wool daily). A Sheep's wool can be shorn with shears, which may be purchased at Marnie's Ranch.
Pigs
Lives in a Deluxe Barn. They can be purchased at Marnie's Ranch for 16,000g. Pigs will find truffles after being let outdoors. Truffles can be used in the oil maker to make truffle oil. Collection will be affected by Foraging Skill, not Farming Skill. Pigs don't leave the Barn come Winter time, therefore they will not find any truffles during Winter. Pigs have a 0.2% chance to dig up a Truffle Crab instead of a Truffle.
Ostriches
Lives in any sized Barn. Ostriches can be hatched by placing an ostrich egg into an ostrich incubator, taking 9d 10h in-game to incubate. Ostriches produce an ostritch egg every 7 days.
An ostrich egg can be initially found by solving Journal Scrap #10, or as a random drop from the chests in the Volcano Dungeon.

Fishing

Fish may be caught in oceans, lakes, rivers, and certain underground locations. Most fish can be caught with a fishing pole. The others require a crab pot to catch. Although some fish spawn only at certain times of the day, during certain seasons, or during certain weather, all fish can be caught regardless of weather, time, or season using some magic bait.

There are 5 legendary fish found in Stardew Valley. Legendary fish are caught in specific places and have a high difficulty. These fish are shown wearing a crown during the Fishing Minigame. Legendary fish can only be caught once per save file. The Lost Book named "Secrets of the Legendary Fish" contains the seasons & locations of the legendary fish. Good luck :P

Artisan Goods

Artisan goods are items that can be created using artisan equipment. They can take anywhere from a few hrs to a few days (in-game) to be processed. For most artisan goods, the star quality of the ingredients used is ignored (except for mayonnaise made from an ostrich egg, smoked fish, and sometimes cloth). Although cloth does not have a star quality itself, it is affected by the quality of the wool used, with higher quality wool resulting in higher chances to produce 2 cloth instead of 1.

One artisan good, the Piña Colada, can be obtained only from the Island Resort on Ginger Island on Sundays when Gus tends the bar there.

Bee House

The bee house is a type of production machine that produces wild honey every 4 days (except in the Winter, or if placed in a greenhouse). The bee house can produce unique types of honey when it is in range of a blooming flower (within 5 tiles in any cardinal direction, which may/may not be grown in garden pots; the closest flower takes priority).

Processing Machines

There are 9 different processing machines available, each performing a different process:

Cask
A cask takes specific products and ages them to increase quality and value. The aging speed varies between products. Casks may age the following:
  • Fruit (any) into wine (takes 2 seasons of aging to go from basic quality wine to iridium quality)
  • Hops into Pale Ale
  • Wheat into Beer
  • Honey into Mead
  • Milk or large milk into cheese
  • or Goat milk or large goat milk into goat cheese
Cheese Press
Processes milk orgoat milk into cheese or goat cheese, respectively, each taking 3h in-game to process (faster than processing cheeses in a cask). It's good to know that using a large milk/large goat milk will always give gold star quality echees/goat cheese, while using regular milk/goat milk will always produce regular quality cheeses. A cheese press may process:
  • Milk or large milk into cheese
  • or Goat milk or large goat milk into goat cheese
Keg
Processes products into beverages (faster processing time compared to a cask). May process:
  • Wheat into Beer
  • Rice into vinegar
  • 5 coffee beans into 1 coffee
  • Tea leaves into green tea
  • Anyvegetable or any positive energy foraged good (other than mushrooms) into juice
  • Honey into mead
  • Hops into Pale Ale
  • Or any fruit into Wine
Mayonnaise Machine
Processes any egg into mayonnaise in 3hrs. May process an:
  • Egg, large egg, ostrich egg or golden egg into mayonnaise (using a golden egg will produce three jars of gold star quality mayo, while an ostrich egg produces 10 jars of mayo at once with the same star quality as the ostrich egg used).
  • Duck eggs into duck mayonnaise
  • Void eggs into void mayonnaise
  • Or a dinosaur eggs into dinosaur mayonnaise
Oil Maker
Processes various products into oil. May process one:
  • Sunflower into oil (processes in 1hr in-game)
  • Corn into oil (in roughly 16hrs)
  • Sunflower seeds into oil (in 2 days)
  • Or a truffle into truffle oil (in 6hrs)
Preserves Jar
Processes one of any vegetable into a pickle (at a time), & fruit into jam. May process one:
  • Vegetable (any), mushroom (any, except red mushrooms) or any positive energy foraged good into a pickle
  • Fruit (any) into jelly
  • Sturgeon roe into caviar
  • Or regular roe into aged roe
Dehydrator
Dries fruit or mushrooms, taking 1 day to dry each. May dry a handful (5) of:
  • Mushrooms (any, except red mushrooms) into a pack of dried mushrooms
  • Fruit (any, except grapes, since they dry into raisins) into dried fruit
  • Or grapes into raisins
Loom
Weaves Cloth into Wool (takes 4hrs in-game to weave). It's good to know that cloth is one of the only artisan goods in the game which are affected by the quality of its raw input.
Fish Smoker
Smokes any fish using one piece of coal to make smoked fish (which is worth double of unsmoked, raw fish), taking 50m (minutes) in-game to smoke. The quality of the fish is preserved.

This webpage contains tables for each processing machine, including the ingredients required, their description, energy/health, the processing time for products & their base sell price/quality, etc.

Cooking

Cooking is a game mechanic that becomes available after upgrading The Farmhouse for the first time. After reaching Foraging Lvl. 3, a cookout kit may be used to cook a dish anywhere.

For food in general (within Stardew Valley), only one food’s buffs can be active at a time, so eating a food with buffs will wipe out any active food buffs and replace them with the buffs from the newly eaten food. If a newly eaten food has no buffs, any existing buffs will remain in effect. Drink buffs are handled separately, so buffs from one drink (Triple Shot Espresso, Coffee, Green Tea or Ginger Ale) can be stacked with one food’s buffs. Using Qi seasoning positively impacts various aspects of dishes.

A minimum of 88 different types of items are needed to cook all 81 dishes in the game, including:

  • 26 crop products
  • 13 foraged goods
  • 4 tree fruits
  • 2 animal products
  • 4 artisan goods
  • maple syrup, squid ink, & moss
  • 17 fished items
  • 8 crab pot items
  • 5 items bought from Pierre's General Store
  • & 6 dishes used as ingredients for other dishes

Good luck, gourmet chef :P

Artifact Spots

They may just look like little wriggling worms, but these Artifact Spots are guaranteed to provide an item when dug up with a hoe (other tools do not work). Artifact Spots can be found in various tillable (but fallow) tiles on maps all over Stardew Valley, including Ginger Island. The possible contents of an Artifact Spot are controlled by the map where it is located. Up to 16 Spots can also be created in a given area by using a treasure totem. Artifact Spots are much more common in Winter, when they can also yield snow yams & winter roots.

Artifact Spots may contain:

  • A Lost Book (cannot be found on the Farm) - There are 21 Lost Books total; They provide info related to the game.
  • An artifact - There are 42 artifacts total. They may also be acquired through other means.
  • A winter root or a snow yam (only Winter)
  • Qi beans (during & after Qi's Crop Quest)
  • Rice shoots (only Spring)
  • Bone fragments (during/after completing Gunther's Special Order "Fragments of the past") - May be used in a Bone Mill or for trading.
  • Ore
  • Coal
  • Stone
  • Clay
  • A secret note
  • Warp totem: Farm/Mountains/Beach (starting Summer 1, Year 1)

Each Artifact Spot can also spawn in addition to the above (and to each other):

  • 1-2 mystery box(es) (0-11.25% chance, depending on daily luck after the player has viewed the Mr. Qi plane cutscene)
  • A golden animal cracker (0.8-1% chance depending on daily luck after claiming Farming Mastery).
  • Either "Bait And Bobber", "Combat Quarterly", "Mining Monthly", "Stardew Valley Almanac", or "Woodcutter's Weekly" (0.54% chance overall, 0.11% chance to obtain each one).
  • A random cosmetic item (1.8% chance)

During the Desert Festival, each Artifact Spot will also spawn between 3 - 6 calico eggs.

Ginger Island holds various other items that may be found underneath Artifact Spots.

Villagers

Villagers are NPCs in Stardew Valley. They are citizens who live in and around Pelican Town. Each Villager has a daily routine, so they can be located in different sections of town depending on the in-game time of the day and weather. You may/may not give various items as gifts to a certain Villager, and may/may not marry certain Villagers, as well. Each Villager has unique likes & dislikes, and will respond to gifts differently. A sortable table of gifts relevant to each Villager in Stardew Valley may be found here. Villagers also sometimes provide quests.

Universally Loved Gifts

Universally loved gifts are items that are guaranteed each villager loves to receive as a gift, with a couple exceptions. These items are guaranteed to give the most friendship points (+80), but may be difficult to obtain. In general, when given a loved gift, villagers usually respond with a heart dialogue bubble. A Golden Pumpkin, Magic Rock Candy, Pearl, Prismatic Shard, & Rabbit's Foot are universally loved gifts, with the exception of Haley hating Prismatic Shards, and Penny hating Rabbit's Feet. Stardrop Tea grants +250 friendship points (equal to +1 heart) when given to any villager, and is not subjected to the weekly gift limit. If given on a villager's birthday (or at the Feast of the Winter Star) it gives +750 points (+3 hearts).

Robin's Shop (Carpenter's Shop)

Robin's Shop (disregarding her house, which is the shop) is closed on Tuesdays (except when it rains), at 4:00pm on Fridays, and always on Thursday, Summer 18th. The shop is also closed on all Festival Days, as well as when she's working on The Farm. The Carpenter's Shop is located in the Mountain area north of Pelican Town. Robin sells wood, stone, and furniture. She can also upgrade & paint the Farmhouse & buildings, and construct new Farm Buildings. She will buy building-related resources, namely clay, fiber, hardwood, wood, and stone.

Museum

There are 53 minerals & 42 artifacts (& 21 Lost Books) that can be donated, totalling to 95 items that can be donated to the Museum. Good luck :P

Adventurer's Guild

To access the Adventurer's Guild, the player must first complete the story quest "Initiation". Once completed, the Guild is open daily from 2pm to 2am (except Festival Fays when the door is locked). The Adventurer's Guild is located east of the Mines (and is Marlon and Gil's house). There you can purchase weapons, boots, and rings. Marlon will also buy weapons, boots, and rings, as well as monster loot from the player.

A list of how many of certain types of Monsters killed is stored on the wall to the right of Marlon's desk. There's 12 Goals to reach, good luck :P.
